Context

In an era of rapid AI advancement, the interfaces we use to interact with AI lagged behind, constraining the authentic potential of human-ai collaboration.

Hunch emerged from two crucial insights: first, that AI's capabilities far exceed what current chat interfaces allow, and second, that elite knowledge workers and AI early adopters were eager to leverage AI more effectively to automate and accelerate their existing tasks.

Today, working with AI often feels like texting an intern—low bandwidth and limited in scope—or like mapping out a tedious business process with high latency and constant context switching. We envisioned Hunch to change that.

My role as Founding Product Designer encompassed driving the product strategy and vision, conducting user research, brand & visual design, designing the core user experience and interface paradigm, collaborating closely with engineers, and leading prototyping efforts to validate concepts and interactions. This case study covers a few of the key decisions and challenges in building Hunch.
