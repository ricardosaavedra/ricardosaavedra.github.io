My background is situated on the intersection of interaction design and HCI in the context of software development and new media applications. Over the past 10 years, I’ve been involved in a variety of projects ranging from open source tools for the Web 2.0 to ubiquitous computing, experimental software architectures, mobile OS, IoT protocols, wearables, simulations environments, and decentralized applications. Throughout this period, I’ve engaged with hardware and software in its opposite extremes, both abstracting its components into user interfaces and products and designing systems in which code itself is used to interface with open sound protocols for music creation and artistic expression.

Over the past 3 years, my work and research have been focused on investigating DLT, mechanism design, cryptography, distributed computation and how they add up to enable the creation of alternative architectures, economic models, and consequentially more open and distributed ecosystems. I have advised and contributed to various projects in the blockchain space such as Pando, an open-source protocol that enables new distributed ownership models, and Status.im, one of the first mobile messaging apps to be built entirely on Ethereum. More recently, my research has investigated more closely the intersection of governance, consensus mechanisms, and simulations models. As a result, I co-founded Sybl, an open simulation platform that seeks to connect computational models from different domains together (such as insurance, urban systems, and climate) to create more complex and inclusive realities across different actors.
