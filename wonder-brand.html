Problem

Wonder.me is a product that seeks to… After the initial growth phase that got the product being used by millions of users worldwide I led a series of projects to rethink the product and visual experience to define solid foundations to help set wonder for a future to come. As a principal designer, I led strategy and oversaw branding, and visual design guidelines across the product experience.

Role

I led product strategy and research, and oversaw art direction for the completion of our brand guidelines in partnership with Design Studio. The project lasted for a period of 5 months with the Brand being only the first of 3 major design/product efforts (product design system, and Wonder's new product iteration being the two consecutive projects).

Immersion and sense making

During this process, I gathered major product stakeholders to participate in a series of workshops, and activities to understand, reflect, on product learnings. We look at industry trends, user feedback, and compiled many insights into larger areas that helped us to identify essential aspects of the project experience.

"
Gatherings are communities where everybody is connected to everybody
"

We identified a set of 6 principles from our interviews with a wide range of users, NPS feedback, and product reviews.

Principles emerged from our analysis. But during workshops we expanded on fundamentals to help us find three different paths we could push our fundamentals. Each leading into its own strategic and visual territory.

Synthesis: converging into a singular idea

We needed to refine and focus our brand strategy into one singular idea that resonated internally and externally. We worked from core values into a core brand proposition. As a summary of our workshops, we condensed our product purpose on one core idea

"
We innovate on technologies that empower everyone to shape incredible gatherings, that leave people energized
"
