Currently, Ricardo leads the development and research of Sybl: an open simulation platform in which previously isolated computational models can be connected, combined, and averaged in one simulation space. The platform is designed to accelerate the role simulations could play in the near future, while addressing collaboration and interoperability challenges between multiple users and domains. Applications of Sybl include: real-time digital twins of complex systems, multi-stakeholder simulation environments , and a robust environment for AI research and computational emergence.

Prior to that, Ricardo collaborated with policy makers, architects, and urban planners developing SOL (Sonic Ontological Language). Deployed as a sonic sensing apparatus at a city scale, the system decodes and categorizes the frequency spectrum to better understand how different agents interrelate acoustically in the urban environment. The semantic database seeks to inspire new design practices in which humans are one of the many users of urban infrastructures. By introducing new policies and better regulating the sonic spectrum, SOL suggest that the preservation of biodiversity is also viable in highly human populated areas, shedding a new light on the antropocene discourse and the role of sound as a carrier of complex interactions between different agents.

As a designer and researcher, Ricardo has been involved on the development of multiple P2P projects and networks. Most recently he helped to develop Pando Network a versioning control system that seeks to decentralize ownership and copy rights. Prior to that he worked helping Status.im to built one of the most secure and decentralized chat applications on the blockchain, while also contributing for the design and testing of the wallet experience and the Dapp environment, which connected users to web3 apps on their mobile devices. While at status, he researched ways to simplify the writing of smart contracts with visual programming languages, enabling common users to write and deploy smart contracts without knowing solidity or other Smart Contract frameworks.

Combining strategic foresight, trends analyses, and user insight, Ricardo oversaw the exploration of alternative futures and product concepts through videos and high-fidelity prototypes to carry the vision across teams. Working alongside designers, HCI researchers, and engineers, Ricardo led the main accessibility strategy for Samsung’s devices and services; managed the development of innovative wearable hardware and image processing techniques; and was a major contributor to multiple future OS concepts. In collaboration with other cross-functional teams, Ricardo worked on envisioning alternative system architectures, services, and communication protocols for device-to-device connectivity (ioT, SmartThings).
