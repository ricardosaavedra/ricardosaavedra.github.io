Sybl

Open Simulation

2019

Sybl is an open simulation platform in which previously isolated computational models can be connected, combined, and averaged in one simulation space. The platform is designed to accelerate the role simulations could play in the near future, while addressing collaboration and interoperability challenges between multiple users and domains. Applications of Sybl include real-time digital twins of complex systems, multi-stakeholder simulation environments, and a robust environment for AI research and computational emergence.

Explainable AI

GPT-2

2019

Explainable AI is a field of study focused on producing more explainable models while maintaining a high level of learning performance (prediction accuracy) to enable human users to understand, appropriately trust, and effectively manage the emerging generation of artificially intelligent agents.

Nested Abstraction Modeling (NAM) is an experimental machine learning technique designed to amplify the characteristics and biases present convolutional neural networks. The approach works by priming a trained model with random inputs, abstracting its outputs before training a second model (1/200th of the original size). The first proof of concept was done in early 2019 using the newly released GPT-2 model by Open Ai. GPT-2’s main objective is to predict the next word, given all of the previous words within a text. Trained on 40GB of internet text, the model produces highly readable and comprehensible responses based on given text input, while keeping its biases and ideological inclinations inaccessible to its creators and users.

NAM works by priming GPT-2 with various text inputs, and further processing its output using techniques such as natural language processing, and sentiment analyses. The data obtained is then used as training data for a 2nd model (nested architecture). While far from state-of-the-art, the model obtained from this process suggests the possibility to translate mathematical logic into abstractions more human-readable (language, visual environments, network graphs), and therefore providing a better glimpse into the original model’s behavior and tendencies under control conditions.

NAM’s findings and a new working prototype are currently being written into a research paper to be released by the end of 2019.

SOL

Machine Listening

2019

SOL (Sonic Ontological Language) emerged out of a working collaboration with policymakers, architects, and urban planners. Deployed as a sonic sensing apparatus at a city scale, the system decodes and categorizes the frequency spectrum to better understand how different agents interrelate acoustically in the urban environment. The semantic database seeks to inspire new design practices in which humans are one of the many users of urban infrastructures. By introducing new policies and better regulating the sonic spectrum, SOL suggests that the preservation of biodiversity is also viable in highly human-populated areas, shedding new light on the Anthropocene discourse and the role of sound as a carrier of complex interactions between different agents.

Pando

Protocol Design

2018

A major problem with open-source licensing is the inability to provide a sustainable ecosystem for creators. Very often, open-source creations switch to a proprietary licensing format after it's release, with little to no way of tracing/rewarding its original contributors. Pando is designed to address this major issue, as well as enable new and more open modes of collaboration and distributed ownership models among content producers. After being released on the network, content can be forked, merged, and receive new contributions, all while maintaining a semantic relationship of its dependencies. Pando seeks to expand practices adopted by the open-source community to a wider range of creative endeavors, such as publishing, intellectual propriety, and software. The core difference from platforms like Github is the introduction of governance mechanisms that are contribution-based (DAOs), enabling contributors to own parts of the repository they contribute to, and consequentially being rewarded as it gets incorporated into new creations.

 

I joined the project in 2018 to contribute to mechanism design, live simulations of network nodes, visual identity, and interface requirements for the beta launch. You can check out the Github repository, or read the Whitepaper. Pando is built on top of IPFS, AragonOS and the Ethereum blockchain.
