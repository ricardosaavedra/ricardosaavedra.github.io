Ricardo Saavedra
Work / Info

Ricardo Saavedra is an interface designer and HCI researcher based in Berlin. His design research explores new visualization methods for machine learning interpretability (XAI), alternative approaches to urban sensing, simulation environments, and portable tools for the governance of virtual worlds (Metagov).

Ricardo earned his Master’s Degree in Interaction Design from PUC (Rio de Janeiro, Brazil), followed by an MFA in computer music and computational art from Mills College (Oakland, CA), where he designed and built various interactive human-machine systems for artistic expression. Ricardo often gives workshops and lectures about designing with AI, critical software, and design theory.

Ricardo has been actively involved in the Web3 space and participated in the development of new DAO frameworks such as Pando Network (design and research) and Status.im (UX research and design strategy). His past endeavors include leading a design team at Samsung’s R&D (San Francisco, CA) on the development of multiple consumer products such as wearables, tablets, and mobile operating systems, and mobile development and design at Globo.com (the largest media group in Latin America).
