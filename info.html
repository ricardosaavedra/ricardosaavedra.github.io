Ricardo Saavedra is an interface designer and HCI researcher based in Berlin, working with computation as a medium to investigate the collisions between art, science, technology, and politics. His research explores new visualization methods for machine learning, alternative approaches for urban sensing, open simulation tools, and data visualization as self-governing mechanisms for P2P networks. Ricardo has been actively involved in the Web3 space and has participated in the development of new DAO frameworks such as Pando Network (design and research), and Status.im (UX research and design strategy). Ricardo received his masters in Interaction Design from (PUC, Brazil), followed by an MFA in computer music and computational art from Mills College (Oakland, CA), where he designed and built various interactive human-machine systems for artistic expression. Ricardo often gives workshops and lectures about designing AI systems, critical software, and design theory.
