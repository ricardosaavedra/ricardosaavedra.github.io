Work / Info

Ricardo Saavedra is an interface designer and HCI researcher based in Berlin, working with computation as a medium to investigate collisions between art, science, technology, and politics. His research explores new visualization methods for machine learning, alternative approaches to urban sensing, open simulation tools, and visualizing governance mechanisms in P2P networks. Ricardo is actively involved in the Web3 space and participated in the development of new DAO frameworks such as Pando Network (design and research) and Status.im (UX research and design strategy). His past endeavors include leading a design team at Samsung’s R&D (San Francisco, CA) on the development of multiple consumer products such as wearables, tablets, and mobile operating systems, and mobile development and design at Globo.com (the largest media group in Latin America). Ricardo earned his Master’s Degree in Interaction Design from PUC (Rio de Janeiro, Brazil), followed by an MFA in computer music and computational art from Mills College (Oakland, CA), where he designed and built various interactive human-machine systems for artistic expression. Ricardo often gives workshops and lectures about designing AI systems, critical software, and design theory.
